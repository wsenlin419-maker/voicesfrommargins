The Initiative

About

Founded April 25, 2026, Margins & Meaning is an independent research initiative dedicated to documenting and revitalizing endangered languages, while examining how marginalized communities are shaped through language and narrative—and how these processes translate into structural inequality.

The project is grounded in the belief that inequality is not only material, but also discursive: it is produced, reinforced, and contested through the ways people speak, are represented, and are heard.

Methodologically, the project adopts an interdisciplinary approach, combining sociolinguistic fieldwork, media and discourse analysis, and quantitative inquiry to connect micro-level experiences with broader structural patterns.

Areas of Focus

Language & Identity

Linguistic hybridity and identity formation in historically marginalized regions.

Media & Narrative

Narrative framing and visual representation in China–Africa media contexts.

Voice & Inequality

The relationship between language, voice, and structural economic inequality.

Preservation & Revitalization

Documenting endangered dialects and oral traditions, and supporting community-based language revitalization.
